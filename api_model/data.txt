**How does the Russian higher education system work?**
The Russian education system is a multistage process that encourages lifelong learning. This system includes compulsory general education (kindergartens and schools) and professional education (colleges, technical schools, universities). In turn, professional education is divided into secondary and higher education. Professional development and retraining programs are considered the final educational stage.
**What degrees are part of the Russian higher education system?**
Russia has adopted the Bologna Process, which is a three-cycle educational system consisting of an undergraduate degree (4 years) and a Master's degree (2 years). Additionally, some universities still offer specialist programs, a level that only exists in Russia. Specialist programs provide a more focused education over 5–6 years that emphasizes practical skills.
**What is the structure of a Master's degree in Russia?**
The Master's Degree is the second stage of higher education in the Bologna Process. To be admitted into a Master's program, applicants must have a Bachelor's or Specialist's degree. These programs are generally more research-oriented, providing students with an in-depth understanding of their chosen subject. Master's programs usually last two years and focus mainly on the Student's ability to independently grasp the material.
**What is a postgraduate program in Russia?**
Postgraduate programs are mainly for training research and teaching personnel. To be admitted into a postgraduate program, applicants must have a Master's or Specialist's Degree. These programs include educational aspects, pedagogical and practical work, and research. Graduate students must choose a scientific area and research topic for their dissertation.
In-person, full-time programs are at least 3 years, while part-time programs are at least 4. At the end of the program, successful candidates receive a postgraduate diploma with a qualification ("Researcher", "Research Teacher"). After defending their dissertation, graduate students are awarded a Candidate of Science degree, which is the first of two doctoral-level scientific degrees. Candidates of Sciences are then eligible for the Doctor of Sciences degree (the second doctoral-level scientific degree), which is awarded following the defense of a doctoral dissertation.
**What is the Point-Grade System (БРС) at UrFU?**
The Point-Grade System is a digital mark for evaluating students' learning activities and achievements. It is a system for determining the level of student success based on the cumulative principle of assessing academic performance and its results.

**What sports activities are available at UrFU?**
The Ural Federal University Sports Club organizes sports activities at the university. There are a total of 32 sports teams at the university. The Sports Club organizes sports sections, sports facilities, events such as Cross-Country Race of the Nation, International Student Sports Day, May Walk, works with the university teams, takes standards of physical culture and sports complex "Ready for Labor and Defense". At the end of the calendar year there is a "New Year's podium" where the best athletes in various categories are awarded.
**What creative activities are available at UrFU?**
The Student Art Center hosts various events for the university's groups, produces performances, writes scripts for events and administers venues. There are many creative groups at UrFU, such as hip-hop and folk dance, academic singing and pop vocal, three theaters, cheerleading, and even a drawing school.
**What is the UrFU Welcome Center?**
Welcome Center is a student tourism cluster of Ural Federal University. A joint team of representatives from several student organizations of Ural Federal University, including the Student Union and the Council for Updating and Promoting the Cultural and Historical Heritage of the Urals, are working in the Welcome Center. The structure of the cluster includes many opportunities for the development of Ural Federal University students: traveling around the Urals, organization of cultural events, adaptation of non-resident and foreign students in Ekaterinburg, actualization and promotion of Urals culture and history, development of eco-culture in the youth environment, soft skills development, and mass media.
**What is the Creative Opportunity Fair at UrFU?**
The Creative Opportunities Fair is traditionally held at the beginning of the academic year to tell students about the kinds of creative activities available at the university. Here you can meet talented students, watch creative teams perform, and become a part of one of them. The Opportunity Fair is organized by the Ural Federal University Student Art Center. The event takes place over three school days during the big break.
**What is the UrFU ESports league (USL)?**
UrFU ESports league (USL) is a project of the organizers of the CITADEL tournament series. USL includes championships in cybersports, programming, and other thematic events. This project has become a single platform for tournaments, educational programs, courses, and other events. Students are involved in organizing and conducting cybersports events throughout the Sverdlovsk Region.
**What is the Rifey Tourist Club at UrFU?**
Rifey Tourist Club deals with all kinds of tourism: mountain, water, hiking, and skiing tours. The club has a climbing section, a hiking and skiing section, and a section of sports tourism. The Tourist Club was founded in 1971 and has trained dozens of athletes and candidates for the master of sports.
**What is the Romantic Tourist Club at UrFU?**
Tourist Club "Romantic" is a meeting place for lovers of travel and everything related to them. The club engages in tourism in all its forms since 1951, including tourist meetings, category tours (hiking, mountain, water, cycling, etc.), multiathlon, weekend hikes, and cultural events.
**What is the Lingua-T Linguistic Theater at UrFU?**
Lingua-T is a unique polyglot theater that performs plays in foreign languages with consecutive translations into Russian. It is the only polyglot theater in Russia that performs plays in their original language with consecutive translation, making the language a full participant in the action.
**What are the benefits of joining Lingua-T Linguistic Theater at UrFU?**
Lingua-T offers the opportunity to learn languages with teachers and through theatrical performances, try your hand as an actor, participate in trips to festivals in Russia and abroad, and broaden your horizons by acquainting yourself with the works of foreign authors.
